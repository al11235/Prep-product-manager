# Estimation / Market Sizing Questions

Target 5–7 minutes per problem. Show structured thinking, not perfect numbers.

## Classic Fermi Problems
1. How many gas stations are there in the United States?
2. How many piano tuners are in Chicago?
3. How much revenue does a single Starbucks store generate per year?
4. How many iPhones are sold in the US per year?
5. What's the total addressable market for electric scooters in NYC?

## Product-Relevant Estimation
6. How many daily active users does Amazon's search have?
7. How much storage does YouTube need per day for new uploads?
8. How many Alexa queries happen per day in the US?
9. What's the market size for grocery delivery in the US?
10. How many rides does Uber complete globally per day?

## Framework for Estimation

1. **Clarify** — What exactly are we estimating? Any constraints?
2. **Break it down** — Decompose into smaller, estimable pieces
3. **Estimate each piece** — Use anchors you know (US population, households, etc.)
4. **Calculate** — Do the math step by step, out loud
5. **Sanity check** — Does the final number feel reasonable? Compare to known benchmarks.

## Useful Anchors
- US population: ~330 million
- US households: ~130 million
- Global internet users: ~5 billion
- US smartphone penetration: ~85%
- Average US household income: ~$75K
- Hours in a year: ~8,760

## Tips
- Round aggressively — 330M → 300M is fine
- State your assumptions explicitly
- Show your work — interviewers care about process, not the exact number
- If you get stuck, try top-down AND bottom-up approaches
- End with: "So my estimate is X. To sanity check, that means Y per person, which feels reasonable."
