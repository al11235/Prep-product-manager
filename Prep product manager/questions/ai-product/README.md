# AI Product Questions — New for 2025–2026

This is the newest interview category. FAANG companies now expect AI fluency from all PMs.

## Core Principle
You don't need to be an ML engineer. You need to demonstrate:
- You understand what AI can and can't do today
- You can scope AI features realistically
- You think about failure modes, trust, and measurement
- You can communicate trade-offs to both engineers and executives

---

## Product Design (AI)
1. Design an AI-powered writing assistant for Gmail. What are the key product decisions?
2. How would you design a conversational AI feature for customer support?
3. Design an AI-powered search experience for an e-commerce site. How is it different from traditional search?
4. You're PM for Alexa. How would you use LLMs to improve the experience?
5. Design an AI feature that helps doctors with diagnosis. What are the constraints?

## Execution (AI)
6. How would you measure success for an AI recommendation engine?
7. Your AI chatbot has a 15% hallucination rate. How do you decide if it's ready to ship?
8. How would you A/B test an AI feature where outputs vary each time?
9. Users report your AI feature is "creepy." How do you investigate and respond?
10. How do you set quality thresholds for an AI feature? (precision vs. recall trade-off)

## Strategy (AI)
11. Should your company build its own foundation model or use an API (OpenAI, Anthropic)?
12. How do you think about competitive moats in AI-powered products?
13. What's the risk of shipping AI too early (trust damage) vs. too late (market loss)?
14. How would you prioritize AI investments across a product portfolio?

## Ethics & Responsibility
15. Your AI hiring tool shows bias against certain demographics. What do you do?
16. How do you balance personalization with privacy in an AI product?
17. Your AI generates content that could be harmful. How do you design guardrails?
18. How do you communicate AI limitations to users without destroying trust?

## Technical Understanding (PM-level)
19. Explain the difference between a rule-based system and ML. When would you choose each?
20. What's the difference between supervised and unsupervised learning? Give a product example of each.
21. How do training data quality issues manifest as product problems?
22. What are the cost/latency trade-offs of using large vs. small models?

## Framework for AI Product Questions

### ADAPT Framework (for AI product design)
| Step | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **A** — Assess the AI capability | What can the model actually do? What are its limits? |
| **D** — Define the user problem | Is AI the right solution, or is a simpler approach better? |
| **A** — Architect the experience | How does the user interact with AI output? What's the UX? |
| **P** — Plan for failure | What happens when AI is wrong? How do you maintain trust? |
| **T** — Test and measure | How do you evaluate quality? What metrics matter? |

## Tips for BIE → PM (AI Questions)
- You likely work with ML models or their outputs already — reference this
- Show you understand data quality → model quality → product quality pipeline
- Mention evaluation metrics you've seen: precision, recall, F1, NDCG, etc.
- Connect to business: "A 5% improvement in model accuracy translates to X in revenue"
- Don't over-engineer — interviewers want product judgment, not ML architecture
