# AARRR (Pirate Metrics) Framework

Use this for "How would you measure success?" or "What metrics would you track?" questions.

## The Funnel

| Stage | Meaning | Example Metrics |
|-------|---------|-----------------|
| **A** — Acquisition | How do users find you? | Traffic, sign-ups, app installs, CAC |
| **A** — Activation | Do they have a good first experience? | Onboarding completion, first key action, time-to-value |
| **R** — Retention | Do they come back? | DAU/MAU, D7/D30 retention, churn rate |
| **R** — Revenue | Do they pay? | ARPU, LTV, conversion rate, AOV |
| **R** — Referral | Do they tell others? | NPS, referral rate, viral coefficient |

## How to Use in Interviews

1. **Start with the product goal** — What stage matters most right now?
2. **Pick a North Star metric** — One metric that best captures value delivery
3. **Add supporting metrics** — 2–3 metrics that give fuller picture
4. **Mention guardrail metrics** — What you don't want to hurt while optimizing

## Example

> "How would you measure success for Instagram Reels?"

- **Stage focus:** Retention + Acquisition (growth phase)
- **North Star:** Daily active Reels viewers (retention signal)
- **Supporting:** Reels created per day, avg watch time, share rate
- **Guardrails:** Feed engagement (don't cannibalize), creator burnout (posting frequency)

## Common Pitfalls
- ❌ Listing metrics without explaining WHY they matter
- ❌ Picking vanity metrics (total downloads) over actionable ones (activation rate)
- ❌ Forgetting to mention trade-offs between metrics
- ❌ Not tying metrics back to user value or business outcome
